When Replacement Beats Another Repair

Many Houston systems from the 1990s and early 2000s still limp through summer with compressor patches, capacitor swaps, and coil rinses. At some point the math flips: a correctly sized new system costs less over five to ten years than emergency visits every July.

We walk you through that decision with age, repair history, refrigerant type (including R-22 phase-out realities), and comfort complaints like rooms that never dry out. If repair still wins, we’ll say so. If a new system is the better buy, we sell and install it.

Sized for Houston—Not a Rule of Thumb

Oversized equipment cools the thermostat too fast and shuts off before it wrings humidity out of the air. That “72° but clammy” feeling is classic oversizing in Climate Zone 2A.

Our installs start with a Manual J style load evaluation: insulation, window exposure, attic conditions, and square footage—not “X square feet per ton” from a national rulebook. Dehumidification performance and airflow are part of the design conversation, not an afterthought.

  • Equipment selection matched to your home’s real load
  • Line set, electrical, and pad work handled with the install
  • Thermostat setup and walkthrough at completion
  • Options explained across efficiency tiers and brands
